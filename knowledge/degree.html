The machine-readable contract for the SDIT Bachelor of Arts in Technology: one degree, 120 semester units across eleven threads, a shared two-year Foundation, a concentration (Media, Design, or Engineering) declared at the end of Year 2, the required Crossing, annual portfolio gates against the five institutional outcomes, and a defended original work at the end.

model
Traditional semester units with grades (transcripts require them); the portfolio is the primary artifact. Dual-register design: every course has a catalog name (accreditation- and transfer-legible) and a teaching frame (how it is actually delivered).
structural_philosophy
One degree. Identical baseline for every student in Years 1-2 — the Foundation (zero electives; the shared formation is the product): a common language and framework for everyone. Nothing is declared at the door, because at the door nobody truly knows. At the end of Year 2 each student sharpens their focus by declaring a concentration — Media, Design, or Engineering — chosen from two years of made work; four depth studios in the declared concentration in Years 3-4, plus the Crossing — one full depth studio from outside it, required of everyone.
standing_test
Would the founder genuinely want to take it? If not, redesign or cut.

End of each year: portfolio review + written and oral examination against the five outcomes (Judgment, Command of Intelligence, Craft, Articulation, Breadth of Mind). See knowledge/outcomes.yaml.
ai_policy
AI-native, not AI-dependent: AI tools open and studied everywhere, AND periodic unassisted examinations in writing, mathematics, and code. The institution certifies the human, so it must regularly observe the human unaided.
