Core materials

Everything you need is grouped in one place

Work from left to right, or open each resource in a new tab as you go. The video lectures are embedded directly so the page stays usable while you study.

Read

John Henry Newman, The Idea of a University

Read Chapter V, "Knowledge Its Own End," and note what Newman thinks a university should cultivate in a student.

Open reading
What to look for
  • How Newman separates knowledge from narrow usefulness.
  • What he thinks liberal learning does to the mind.
  • Where his view feels timeless, and where it feels limited.

Watch

Harry Lewis, Excellence with a Soul

A public lecture on what higher education loses when it becomes only credentialing or career sorting.

Open on YouTube
What to listen for
  • Where Lewis says universities succeed, and where they drift.
  • What he thinks education should shape beyond skill.
  • How his critique compares with Newman.

Watch

Michael Sandel, Justice Episode 1

A foundational Sandel lecture that models serious public thinking about moral life, judgment, and disagreement.

Open on YouTube
What to listen for
  • How Sandel turns a question into shared inquiry.
  • What makes the lecture feel active rather than passive.
  • How moral reasoning fits into a liberal education.

Listen

Susan Wolf on meaning in life

This short Philosophy Bites conversation helps connect study, meaning, and the shape of a life worth building.

Open podcast

Practice and reflection

Turn the material into your own position

Do not stop at consumption. The point of Day 1 is to produce language, structure, and questions that belong to you.

Write

Define education in your own words

Write a definition of education without borrowing school language. Name what you believe education is for and what it should change in a person.

Compare

Put Newman, Lewis, and Sandel in conversation

Organize your notes around three headings: where you agree, where you diverge, and what you want to carry forward into this degree.

Map

Draw your worldview

Sketch what matters most to you, how you see the world, and where education sits inside that picture. Use arrows, clusters, symbols, or rough diagrams.

Reflect

Journal toward the person you want to become

Write on three prompts: why you want an education, who you hope to become by the end of this program, and what kind of life you are trying to build.

Optional challenge

Design your ideal university

List ten courses or experiences every student should have. Then write a short manifesto explaining your university's purpose, structure, and soul, including what you would remove from traditional models.

Notes for today

Use the page as a studio, not a checklist

The goal is not to finish quickly. The goal is to start the degree with seriousness, curiosity, and language that already feels your own.

Pace

Slow is allowed

Spend longer with one resource if it opens something important. A strong first day matters more than a fast first day.

Method

Keep friction visible

Highlight what you resist, not just what you agree with. Tension is usually where the real work begins.

Aim

Be truthful before polished

Your early definitions and reflections do not need to be elegant. They need to be honest enough to build on later.
