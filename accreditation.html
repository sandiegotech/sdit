The Plain Truth

The San Diego Institute of Technology is in its founding phase. It is not yet authorized to grant degrees, and nothing on this site is an offer of one. The curriculum published here — the Bachelor of Arts in Technology framework — is an open educational resource, and the road to authorization is being walked in public, with this page as the record.

Until authorization, the honest language is this: take the courses because they're worth taking. The degree comes when the state and the accreditor say it can.

The Road
Incorporate & govern

Nonprofit incorporation, an independent board, audit-capable books. The boring machinery that everything else stands on.

BPPE approval

California's Bureau for Private Postsecondary Education must approve the institution to operate before it can lawfully enroll degree students or promise degrees. Typically 12–24 months.

WSCUC eligibility & candidacy

With a founding cohort operating under BPPE approval, the institution pursues eligibility, then candidacy, with the WASC Senior College and University Commission.

Initial accreditation

At least one graduated class before the review. Realistically, the whole road is 7–10 years from founding — and we'd rather say that plainly than pretend otherwise.

Meanwhile

All of the course material is open and free to take, today. You can read every page without an account. Free general-interest education requires no state approval — that's why the coursework can ship years before the degree can.

We're building a way to log in and save your progress on the site. Until then, you can fork the repository on GitHub, run it locally, and write your answers into your own copy — your fork becomes a portfolio of the work as you go.

The Covenant Total transparency about pre-accreditation status, always. If you ever see degree language on this site before authorization exists, hold us to it.
