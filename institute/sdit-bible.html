The SDIT Bible

San Diego Institute of Technology — Institutional Plan Founding Working Document · June 2026

This is the plan. The companion document — The Degree — is the working framework for the four-year program itself. This document explains why the institution exists, what it is made of, and how it comes into being.

I. The Premise

Industrial-era education was built to produce specialists for a world of stable professions. That world is ending. The institutions that matter next will not be “AI universities” teaching tools that depreciate in eighteen months. They will be institutions that produce people whose value compounds as machine intelligence advances — people with judgment, taste, epistemic self-defense, command of intelligent systems, and the craft instinct to make real things in the physical world.

This is the Post-Industrial Renaissance thesis: technology, art, craft, science, and philosophy are one discipline, artificially divided by the industrial university. SDIT reunites them.

II. The Model

Three lineages, fused:

  • The Renaissance bottega — learning happens inside real work, under masters, in a workshop that ships.
  • Bell Labs / early MIT — deep technical rigor in service of building, not credentialing.
  • The waterman tradition — physical mastery, courage, and judgment as part of the formation of the whole person. San Diego is not where SDIT happens to be; San Diego is the laboratory. The ocean teaches physics. The culture teaches media. The terrain teaches engineering.

III. The Dual-Register Principle

The governing design rule of everything SDIT teaches: keep the names, transform the delivery.

Transcripts, regulators, accreditors, and graduate schools read course titles. So the catalog says Mechanics, Calculus I, Probability & Statistics — and the classroom is Scripps Pier, the ramp, the racing line, the recording studio. The catalog satisfies the system; the teaching frame is the education. The credential is the interface; the portfolio is the truth.

Precedents: Deep Springs (great books + ranch labor, accredited), Olin College (one rigorous degree, entirely project-delivered), Scripps Institution of Oceanography (the ocean as world-class laboratory). Cautionary precedent: Black Mountain College — legendary pedagogy, no financial or accreditation structure, dead. SDIT takes the pedagogy and the structure.

IV. The Three Layers of an SDIT Education

SDIT is not only a degree. It is three concentric layers, each feeding the next:

Layer 1 — Perspectives (open to everyone)

Short, free, standalone lessons — roughly hour-long lectures and films. A master shaper on fin theory. A reading of Book One of the Iliad against a war film. The physics of Black’s Beach. How a song actually reaches the world in 2026. Each one complete in itself, made for people who will never enroll.

Perspectives is simultaneously:

  • The public face — the institution’s ideas, freely given (the MIT OpenCourseWare instinct, but cinematic and place-based rather than filmed lectures).
  • The content engine — produced by faculty and, once cohorts exist, by students in the Culture & Signal thread.
  • The recruiting funnel — the thirty people who watch all of them are the founding cohort.
  • The legal head start — free general-interest education requires no state approval, so Perspectives can begin now, before BPPE, before a campus, before anything.

Layer 2 — Intensives (the bridge)

Short paid programs: a two-week summer intensive, weekend workshops, a single Canon seminar with a build project. Non-degree, small, real. This is where Project One lives — the founding pilot that proves the pedagogy, produces the imagery, and becomes page one of every application. (Regulatory note: avocational and general-interest programs are generally outside BPPE’s degree-granting regime, but verify the exemption boundaries with counsel before charging tuition for anything that sounds vocational.)

Layer 3 — The Degree (the core)

One four-year degree — the Bachelor of Arts in Technology — fully specified in the companion document. Identical baseline for every student in the first two years, so the artist and the engineer speak the same language; then a sharpened focus — a concentration in Media (things people watch, hear, and feel), Design (things people use), or Engineering (things that work on their own) — declared at the end of Year 2, from experience rather than at the door. Four depth studios in the declared concentration in the back half, plus the Crossing — one full depth studio from outside it, required of everyone; physical culture throughout; a defended original work at the end. Only eight depth studios in the whole catalog distinguish the concentrations, and one program is the honest accreditation unit. Designed so that the founder would genuinely want to take any of the three — that is the standing test for every course added or cut.

The layers are also the growth sequence: Perspectives now → Intensives next → the Degree when authorized. The institution becomes real in public, in order, with proof at each step.

V. The Five Outcomes

Everything SDIT certifies reduces to five capacities, assessed at annual gates:

  1. Judgment — rigorous reasoning under uncertainty.
  2. Command of Intelligence — directing, building, and evaluating AI systems as a native medium.
  3. Craft — conceiving, designing, and physically realizing working artifacts.
  4. Articulation — writing and speaking with clarity and force.
  5. Breadth of Mind — history, philosophy, literature, science, and living culture as one continuous conversation.

These are the institution’s philosophy and its accreditation evidence file, written once and defended everywhere.

VI. Physical Culture

A graduation-required, non-credit program — the waterman tradition institutionalized. Surfing as the spine; skateboarding, snowboarding, motorcycling, freediving, sailing as core disciplines; jiu-jitsu or boxing as the standing combat practice; and an annual Foreign Term where a visiting practitioner teaches an outside tradition (Muay Thai, rugby sevens, pelota, kendo, Aussie surf lifesaving).

Integration is the point: the board was shaped in Workshop with the math from class, the session was filmed by the Culture & Signal crew, the swell was forecast in Statistics.

The unromantic commitment: certified instructors, lifeguard/EMT coverage, counsel-reviewed waivers, formal progressions and certifications, insurance from day one. Risk management is what separates a program from a lawsuit.

VII. The Accreditation Pathway

The realistic arc for a private degree-granting institution in California seeking WSCUC accreditation:

Stage 0 — Incorporate & Govern (Year 0). Nonprofit incorporation; a genuinely independent governing board (including at least one member who has been through a WSCUC process); audit-capable books from the first dollar.

Stage 1 — BPPE Approval to Operate (Years 0–2). California’s Bureau for Private Postsecondary Education must approve the institution before it can lawfully enroll degree students or promise degrees. Full package: catalog, faculty, facilities, financials. Budget 12–24 months. Layers 1 and 2 operate legally throughout this stage.

Stage 2 — Operate & WSCUC Eligibility (Years 2–4). Enroll the founding cohort under BPPE approval; pursue WSCUC eligibility (inquiry → preliminary review → formal review of fit and financial standing). Eligibility is a preliminary finding, not a status.

Stage 3 — Candidacy → Initial Accreditation (Years 4–8). First visit within five years of eligibility; substantial compliance can yield Initial Accreditation after one visit, minimal compliance yields Candidacy (max five years); at least one class must graduate before Initial Accreditation review. Realistic total: 7–10 years from founding. Calibration: John Paul the Great Catholic University, San Diego County — eligibility 2007, Initial Accreditation 2015.

The founding-cohort covenant. Pre-accreditation students face limited transferability and no federal aid. The honest play: founding scholarships, total transparency, and the truthful pitch — you are founders of an institution, not customers of a product. There are thirty people in the world who would cross the country to read Homer, shape a board, and build AI systems by the ocean. Perspectives is how they find out the place exists.

VIII. Sequencing — The Standing Order of Operations

  1. Begin Perspectives. Commission and produce the first five — they can be made anywhere, now. The institution becomes real the day the first one is published.
  2. Write the dual-register catalog (the companion document, taken to catalog grade). One artifact: BPPE core, WSCUC core, recruiting pitch.
  3. Seat the board.
  4. Run Project One — the two-week summer intensive: one Double Feature seminar, one ocean-physics module, one board build, filmed by participants, public exhibition. Twelve people. No degree claims.
  5. File BPPE. Then the long road, in public, with proof at every step.
