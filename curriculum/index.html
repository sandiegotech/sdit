The Degree

One Bachelor of Arts in Technology. The first two years are the Foundation — identical for every student, no electives, nothing to choose — so everyone leaves with the same language and framework: the books, the mathematics, the code, the Workshop. Then you sharpen your focus. At the end of Year 2, when you actually know what you're interested in and good at, you declare a concentration.

Media Things people watch, hear, and feel Film, music production, interactive story, generative media — made by someone with the full technical core underneath. See the studios → Design Things people use Interfaces, interaction, fabrication, physical computing — where made things meet people. The bridge concentration, on purpose. See the studios → Engineering Things that work on their own AI systems, embedded sensors, robotics — built by someone who has also read Homer, mixed a record, and cut a film. See the studios →
The Shape
Years 1–2

The Foundation — identical for everyone. The great books, the mathematics of motion and sound, code and machine learning, writing, and the Workshop. You don't have to know what you are yet; these two years are designed to show you.

Years 3–4

Your focus: four depth studios in your concentration, plus the Crossing — one studio from outside it, required. A term inside a real organization, then a capstone defended in public.

Throughout

The Workshop every semester — a project team that ships and exhibits. Annual reviews of your portfolio. Physical culture alongside.

The Timeline
Year 1

The foundation begins. Ends with your work in a public exhibition.

Year 2

The Foundation completes. Ends with the Declaration — you choose your concentration from experience: Media, Design, or Engineering.

Year 3

Your focus begins. A full term inside a real studio, lab, or venture.

Year 4

The Crossing, and a capstone defended in public. You teach a first-year. Graduation.
