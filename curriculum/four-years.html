The Four Years

BA in Technology · 120 units · 8 semesters · concentration declared end of Year 2

First a common language. Then your focus. Two years of shared Foundation — the books, the mathematics, the code, the Workshop — so every student can speak both art and engineering. Then four depth studios in the concentration you choose: Media, Design, or Engineering. And it doesn't start with a mountain: it starts with one ten-minute day.

Years 1–2The Foundation

First, you learn both languages.

Every artist here trains in the engineer's tools, and every engineer in the artist's — these two years are identical for every student, no electives, on purpose. Each course is named for what it actually teaches; the formal catalog name rides along in small print for the transcript people.

Years 3–4Your Focus

Then you sharpen — and ship.

Four depth studios in your concentration, a full term inside a real studio, lab, or venture, the Crossing — one depth studio from outside your concentration, required, because that's the point — and a final work defended in public. The shared spine continues throughout: the Canon, Culture & Signal, the Workshop.

The ConcentrationsDeclared Year 2

Eight studios. Your four.

The depth catalog is eight studios; each concentration prescribes four of them. The lists overlap on purpose — Design shares studios with both neighbors, because design is where made things meet people. The Crossing sends you outside your four, once, required.

Media — things people watch, hear, and feel

  • Film & the Moving Image — camera, edit, story. You ship a short film.ART 320 · 4 units
  • Music Production & Sound Design — the studio as instrument.ART 330 · 4 units
  • Interactive Media & Interfaces — games, installations, the designed experience.ART 310 · 4 units
  • Generative Media & Creative Code — procedure and machine intelligence as artistic material.ART 410 · 4 units

Design — things people use

  • Interactive Media & Interfaces — the designed experience, watched in the hands of real users.ART 310 · 4 units
  • Generative Media & Creative Code — systems that design alongside you.ART 410 · 4 units
  • Mechanical Design & Fabrication — CAD to shop floor; tolerance is a moral category.ENGR 310 · 4 units
  • Embedded Systems & Sensors — physical computing; objects that sense and respond.ENGR 320 · 4 units

Engineering — things that work on their own

  • Machine Learning II / AI Systems — deployment, evaluation, agents, failure modes.CS 310 · 4 units
  • Mechanical Design & Fabrication — CAD to shop floor.ENGR 310 · 4 units
  • Embedded Systems & Sensors — firmware, ocean sensors, instruments that survive saltwater.ENGR 320 · 4 units
  • Robotics & Autonomous Systems — perception, control, autonomy in the physical world.ENGR 410 · 4 units

What you walk out with

A defended original work that exists outside any classroom — a finished film, a released record, a product with real users, a system that runs, a venture that exists — plus four years of exhibited Workshop builds and a portfolio that is the actual credential. The credential is the interface; the portfolio is the truth.
