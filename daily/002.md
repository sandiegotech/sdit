Van Gogh sold almost nothing while he was alive. What kept him going was a twenty-year correspondence with his brother Theo — over six hundred letters about color, failure, money, weather, and why he kept painting anyway. "I am seeking, I am striving, I am in it with all my heart," he wrote. He painted the night sky from a hospital window and mailed his hope to his brother in installments.

## The story

A man paints the sky from the window of the place he went to fall apart. Another sings about a wonderful world during one of his country's ugliest years. A town square fills with Ode to Joy between errands on an ordinary Saturday.

None of them found beauty lying around. All of them *put it there* — and described the world not as it was, but as it could be seen. That's not naivety; it's authorship. Tonight, look up once on purpose. Venus is still where it's supposed to be.
