LIFE-202 — Health, Fitness & Human Performance

Your body is the platform everything else runs on — and most people are running it on default settings.

The Central Question

What is your body actually capable of — and what is the gap between where you are and where you could be?

What This Course Is About

This course is a systematic, evidence-based approach to physical training and recovery. You’ll assess where you actually are, learn the principles of strength, conditioning, mobility, and recovery, and build a training plan you’ll execute and adjust over the course of fifteen days. Alongside the physical practice, you’ll track sleep, nutrition, and subjective performance — learning to read your own data and respond to it intelligently rather than just pushing harder. The goal is a sustainable practice you can maintain well beyond the course.

Why It Matters Now

Cognitive performance, mood, resilience, and creative output are all substantially determined by physical health. In an era of sedentary, screen-heavy work, the people who invest systematically in physical capacity have a genuine edge — not just in the gym, but in everything they do.

By the End of This Course, You Will

  • Assess your current physical baseline across strength, conditioning, mobility, and recovery
  • Design a progressive training plan appropriate for your current level and goals
  • Track and interpret sleep, heart rate, and training metrics to make informed adjustments
  • Understand and apply injury prevention principles for the movements you practice
  • Build habits you can sustain beyond the course — not a 15-day transformation, but a permanent shift

The 15-Day Arc

Week 1 — Assess and Plan

  • Day 1: Baseline assessment — strength, cardiovascular capacity, mobility, and sleep quality
  • Day 2: Movement quality — fundamental patterns, mobility work, and what your body is telling you
  • Day 3: Strength fundamentals — the principles of progressive overload and the movements that matter
  • Day 4: Conditioning zones — aerobic base, threshold work, and how to train each without burning out
  • Day 5: Sleep and recovery — the most underrated performance intervention, with the science behind it

Week 2 — Build and Track

  • Day 6: Nutrition basics — protein, energy balance, and what actually affects performance
  • Day 7: Technique and coaching — how to learn movement patterns and give yourself useful feedback
  • Day 8: Injury prevention — the common failure modes and how to train around your weak points
  • Day 9: Progressions and plateaus — how to keep improving and what to do when you stop
  • Day 10: Metrics and monitoring — what to track, how often, and when to trust the data

Week 3 — Sustain and Integrate

  • Day 11: Stress management and breath — the physiological connection between mental load and physical recovery
  • Day 12: Training around constraints — what to do when life gets in the way of the plan
  • Day 13: Mid-course review — compare your data to your baseline and adjust the plan
  • Day 14: Consolidation — the taper, the long game, and how to protect what you’ve built
  • Day 15: Final assessment and plan forward — measure your progress and design the next phase

What You’ll Make

  • A training plan with weekly structure, progressive overload built in, and clear rationale for every choice
  • A training and recovery log covering all fifteen days, with notes and adjustments
  • A final performance reflection comparing your baseline to your current state and outlining your next twelve weeks

Key Practices

  • Consistency beats intensity — showing up and doing the work matters more than any single session
  • Sleep is training — treat recovery as seriously as you treat the workout
  • Track your data, but trust your body — the log informs the decision; it doesn’t make it
