# LAB-101 — Creative Intelligence Lab

This is a making course — every week you produce something real, with no instructions for what "good" looks like, and that's exactly the point.

## The Central Question

> What happens when you make something with no instructions and have to decide for yourself if it's good?

## What This Course Is About

The Creative Intelligence Lab is a studio course that runs alongside your academic work. You'll tackle a different creative challenge each week — building objects, composing images, writing constraints, designing systems, recording sound — and you'll document your process, not just your output. The goal isn't to make perfect things. It's to develop creative judgment: the ability to set your own standard, know when you've met it, and learn from the gap.

## Why It Matters Now

AI can generate. You need to create. The difference is intentionality, judgment, and the willingness to make decisions no tool can make for you. This lab trains those capacities directly.

## By the End of This Course, You Will

- Work across multiple creative media with genuine curiosity rather than anxiety
- Document your creative process in enough detail to learn from it and share it
- Embrace constraints as generative rather than limiting
- Develop and defend your own standards for what makes something work
- Build a body of small, finished artifacts that show your range

## The 15-Day Arc

**Week 1 — Starting**

- Day 1: Mapping your creative landscape — where you've been and what you're afraid of
- Day 2: Constraints as invitations — make something in 20 minutes using only three elements
- Day 3: Play as method — serious games, structured improvisation
- Day 4: Process documentation — capturing not just what you made but how
- Day 5: First critique — share something rough and learn to talk about it honestly

**Week 2 — Expanding**

- Day 6: Storytelling through images — a sequence of five photographs with no words
- Day 7: Sound and rhythm — a one-minute composition using only found sounds
- Day 8: Design with constraints — make something useful from materials on your desk
- Day 9: Collaboration — create something together that neither of you would have made alone
- Day 10: Creative spaces — how your environment shapes what you make

**Week 3 — Deepening**

- Day 11: Patterns in complexity — find structure in something that appears chaotic
- Day 12: Technology as material — use a digital tool as a physical artist would use a brush
- Day 13: Creative courage — make the thing you've been avoiding
- Day 14: Flow and resistance — working through the places where you get stuck
- Day 15: Creative manifesto — what you believe about making, and the artifact that proves it

## What You'll Make

- A process journal documenting every creative session with notes, sketches, and reflections
- A portfolio of at least ten small finished artifacts across different media
- A creative manifesto: one page stating what you believe about making things and why

## Key Practices

- Document before you polish — the messy process is as important as the finished thing
- Finish something every session, even if it's small
- Give your creative work the same rigor you give your analytical work
