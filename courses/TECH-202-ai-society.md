# TECH-202 — AI & Society

AI is the most consequential general-purpose technology of our lifetimes, and most people deploying it have no idea what could go wrong — this course changes that.

## The Central Question

> Who is responsible when an AI system causes harm — and how do you decide?

## What This Course Is About

This course builds the conceptual toolkit for thinking clearly about artificial intelligence in the real world: what these systems actually do, where they fail, and what individuals, organizations, and governments should do about it. You'll analyze real cases of AI deployment, draft responsible-use policies, and work through the governance questions that don't have clean answers yet. No prior technical knowledge required — but by the end, you'll be able to read a technical paper, translate it for a non-technical audience, and write a policy recommendation that holds up to scrutiny.

## Why It Matters Now

AI is already making consequential decisions in hiring, lending, medical diagnosis, criminal justice, and military targeting. Understanding how these systems work — and how to evaluate whether they're working fairly — is not a specialist skill anymore. It's a citizenship skill.

## By the End of This Course, You Will

- Explain core AI capabilities and failure modes in plain language to any audience
- Analyze a deployed AI system for bias, misuse potential, and governance gaps
- Draft responsible-use policies and evaluation frameworks for an organization
- Track and interpret AI regulation across jurisdictions
- Write decision-ready briefs that give stakeholders real options, not just risk lists

## The 15-Day Arc

**Week 1 — Understanding**

- Day 1: What AI actually is — capabilities, limits, and the terminology that matters
- Day 2: Data, privacy, and the supply chain of AI systems
- Day 3: Bias and fairness — how systems encode and amplify inequity
- Day 4: Safety, alignment, and the cases where things have already gone wrong
- Day 5: Labor, productivity, and the economic disruption that's already happening

**Week 2 — Governance**

- Day 6: Intellectual property, attribution, and what AI does to authorship
- Day 7: Regulation — the EU AI Act, US executive orders, and the global patchwork
- Day 8: Risk management — controls, audits, and meaningful human oversight
- Day 9: Procurement and vendor risk — questions to ask before you deploy
- Day 10: Human-in-the-loop systems — when is oversight genuine and when is it theater?

**Week 3 — Action**

- Day 11: Sector deep dives — AI in health, finance, and education
- Day 12: Incident response and disclosure — what happens when something goes wrong
- Day 13: Writing policies and playbooks — from principles to procedures people will actually follow
- Day 14: Tabletop exercise — walk through a real AI incident scenario as a team
- Day 15: Final brief and policy pack — your complete responsible-use framework

## What You'll Make

- Two decision briefs analyzing real AI deployments for risk, governance, and recommended action
- A responsible-use policy draft with accompanying evaluation checklist
- A tabletop exercise reflection documenting what the scenario revealed and what you'd change

## Key Practices

- Write for decisions — every analysis should end with options, trade-offs, and a recommendation
- Keep humans accountable for outcomes, regardless of what the system decided
- Track the field weekly — AI governance is moving faster than any course can cover
