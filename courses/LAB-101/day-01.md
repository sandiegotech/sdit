# Day 05 — Make Something
**Course:** LAB 101 – Creative Intelligence Lab I

---

## Session Focus

This is the only session in the program with one rule and no reading list.

**The rule:** Make something. Today. By hand, by voice, on paper, in code, in soil — medium doesn't matter. It has to come from you. No prompt from anyone else. No AI starting point. No template. Just you deciding to make a thing, and then making it.

That's it.

But here's why it matters: creativity isn't a talent. It's a practice. And the first step of the practice is showing up and making something even when you don't know if it will be good. Especially when you don't know.

> *"You can't use up creativity. The more you use, the more you have."* — Maya Angelou

---

## What You'll Do Today

**Time:** ~60 minutes
**Output:** One small artifact — anything — that didn't exist before you sat down

---

## Session Flow

### 1. Watch first (10 min)

**Ira Glass on the gap between taste and ability**
[Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X2wLP0izeJE)

Ira Glass is the creator of This American Life. This 2-minute clip is one of the most honest things anyone has ever said about what it feels like to create something when you're starting out.

Watch it. Take one note. Then close everything and go make your thing.

---

### 2. Read — only if you want to, after you make (10 min)

**Austin Kleon — *Steal Like an Artist*, Chapter 1**
[Read at Austin Kleon's site](https://austinkleon.com/steal/)

Save this for after you've made your artifact. It's about where ideas come from — but you'll understand it better if you've just done the making first.

---

### 3. Make your thing (30 min)

Pick one. Make it. Finish it.

**If you write:** A poem (three lines or thirty — your call). A letter to someone you haven't spoken to. A short scene of fiction. A description of a place.

**If you draw or design:** A portrait of someone from memory. A map of a place that matters to you. A pattern based on something from Day 3. A logo for something imaginary.

**If you build or make physically:** Arrange objects into something intentional. Fold paper. Rearrange a shelf. Build something small.

**If you make sound:** Hum or sing something that doesn't exist yet. Record it on your phone. Name it.

**If none of these:** Pick the thing you'd make if nobody would ever see it. Make that.

---

### 4. Sit with it (5 min)

When you're done, put it somewhere you can see it. Sit with it for five minutes without judging it.

Then write two things:
- What were you trying to do?
- What actually happened?

---

## Practice Block

There is no formal practice block today. The making *is* the practice.

After you've made your artifact, write a short reflection answering:

**1. What was the hardest moment?** When did you almost stop, simplify, or give up?

**2. What surprised you?** Something in the process or the output you didn't expect.

**3. Where did the idea actually come from?** Not the polished story. The real one. A memory, a feeling, something you saw this week, a question from Day 1.

---

## AI Lens (Optional)

Show your artifact to your AI assistant and ask it one question:

> "What would you change about this?"

Read the response. Then ask yourself: do you agree? Does it matter whether you agree?

This isn't about whether AI feedback is useful (sometimes it is). It's about noticing your own reaction to being critiqued by something that has no stake in your work.

## Week 1 Reflection

You've finished the first week. Five sessions, five different ways of seeing the world.

Before you move to Week 2, spend 10 minutes with these questions:

**What do you have now that you didn't have on Day 1?**

You have: a manifesto about why education matters, a piece of writing that only you could write, a pattern journal from your own observation, a 10-minute record of pure looking, and one small thing you made.

That's not nothing. That's an archive beginning.

> **Where did your thinking surprise you this week?** Pick one moment — from any of the five sessions — where something shifted. Write it down.

This moment goes in your archive too.

## Hard Problem (Optional)

**The constraint exercise**

Make the same thing again — but with one severe constraint:

- If you wrote something: rewrite it in exactly half the words.
- If you drew something: redraw it using only straight lines.
- If you made something physical: make it again using only one material.
- If you made sound: repeat it at a different tempo.

Constraint is one of the most powerful creative tools there is. What does limiting yourself force you to see about what actually matters in the thing you made?

---

## Notes

- Nothing you make today needs to be good. It needs to be finished.
- "Finished" means: you decided it was done. Not perfect. Done.
- If you're feeling stuck, set a timer for 25 minutes and make something in that window. Whatever exists when the timer goes off — that's your artifact.
- If you make something you hate: good. That's the gap Ira Glass is talking about. Keep going.
