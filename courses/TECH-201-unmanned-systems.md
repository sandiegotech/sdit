# TECH-201 — Unmanned Systems

You are going to build, configure, and fly an unmanned aircraft — and you are going to do it safely, which turns out to be the harder part.

## The Central Question

> What does it mean to operate a system that extends your reach — and your responsibility?

## What This Course Is About

This is a hands-on course in unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) from the ground up. You'll assemble a small multi-rotor platform, configure its avionics and radio links, calibrate its sensors, plan missions, and fly — keeping detailed logs the whole way. Alongside the hardware work, you'll grapple with what it means to operate a system that can go places you can't and do things you can't undo. Safety, spectrum discipline, and honest documentation are not add-ons here; they are the course.

## Why It Matters Now

Unmanned systems are reshaping logistics, agriculture, infrastructure inspection, defense, and emergency response. Engineers and operators who combine systems-level thinking with disciplined field practice — and who can document and communicate what they've done — are genuinely rare and in demand.

## By the End of This Course, You Will

- Assemble and configure a modular UAS platform including airframe, power, avionics, and radio
- Plan and execute safe flight operations using pre-flight checklists and defined safety protocols
- Explain radio link fundamentals and spectrum hygiene; configure and verify failsafe behavior
- Capture, log, and analyze basic telemetry data from field operations
- Produce a complete flight logbook and a final mission report that others can learn from

## The 15-Day Arc

**Week 1 — Build**

- Day 1: Safety, orientation, and airspace basics — the rules before the fun
- Day 2: Airframes and propulsion — frame assembly, motors, props, thrust math
- Day 3: Power systems — batteries, wiring, connectors, soldering, continuity
- Day 4: Avionics and sensors — flight controller installation, IMU, GPS, calibration
- Day 5: Radio links and spectrum — binding receiver, mapping channels, configuring failsafe

**Week 2 — Configure and Fly**

- Day 6: Flight modes and tuning — rate maps, PID basics, first hover parameters
- Day 7: Ground control and telemetry — GCS setup, link test, logging verification
- Day 8: Pre-flight checklists and dry runs — first outdoor operations with safety lead
- Day 9: Hover and patterns — stable hover, square patterns, figure-eights at low altitude
- Day 10: Mission planning — waypoint missions, success metrics, simulator rehearsal

**Week 3 — Operate and Analyze**

- Day 11: Sensors and data — refining GPS/compass, capturing logs during maneuvers
- Day 12: Troubleshooting and recovery — diagnose desync, brownouts, GPS glitches
- Day 13: Payloads and capture — mounting a camera, power management, stabilized footage
- Day 14: EW awareness and spectrum hygiene — interference scenarios, antenna placement
- Day 15: Capstone mission and debrief — execute planned mission, contingency handling, final report

## What You'll Make

- A complete build documentation package: photos, wiring diagram, configuration backup, calibration log
- A flight logbook with a minimum of five entries, each with pre-flight checklist, observations, and post-flight analysis
- A final mission report covering goals, method, results, lessons learned, and next steps

## Key Practices

- Bench, field, debrief — every session runs this cycle and the debrief tightens the next one
- No flight without a completed checklist and a designated safety lead — no exceptions
- Document every build change and every anomaly; the log is part of the work
