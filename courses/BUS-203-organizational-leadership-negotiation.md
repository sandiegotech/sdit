# BUS-203 — Organizational Leadership & Negotiation

Teams fail more often from bad coordination than from bad ideas — this course is about fixing the coordination.

## The Central Question

> What does it take to get people genuinely aligned around something that matters?

## What This Course Is About

This is a practical course in leading small, mission-driven teams and negotiating with integrity. You'll learn how to set direction clearly, design processes that produce decisions without creating bureaucracy, and run negotiations that build long-term relationships instead of burning them. Every concept gets tested in role-play and real scenarios, and you'll leave with concrete artifacts: a team charter, a decision log template, a negotiation framework you can use immediately.

## Why It Matters Now

The organizations building important things in the AI age tend to be small, fast, and cross-disciplinary. The people who can coordinate that kind of work — who can align a small team around an ambiguous goal and make good decisions without perfect information — are the ones driving outcomes.

## By the End of This Course, You Will

- Define goals, roles, and decision rights for a team in writing, before ambiguity causes problems
- Design and run meetings that produce clear decisions and real accountability
- Apply principled negotiation frameworks including interest-mapping and BATNA analysis
- Resolve conflicts constructively without sacrificing relationships or outcomes
- Write leadership memos, team charters, and decision logs that people actually use

## The 15-Day Arc

**Week 1 — Structure**

- Day 1: Goals, roles, and responsibilities — the foundational clarity that prevents most conflicts
- Day 2: Meeting design — agendas, outcomes, and how to run a meeting worth attending
- Day 3: Decision frameworks — RACI, RAPID, one-way vs. two-way doors, and when to use each
- Day 4: Feedback systems — one-on-ones, retrospectives, and the feedback that people actually hear
- Day 5: Written culture — decision logs, memos, and why writing things down multiplies trust

**Week 2 — Negotiation**

- Day 6: Negotiation foundations — interests vs. positions and why the distinction matters
- Day 7: BATNA, reservation price, and ZOPA — the vocabulary of principled deal-making
- Day 8: Tactics and ethics — what works in the short term and what destroys trust
- Day 9: Role-play 1 — a bilateral negotiation, debriefed in detail
- Day 10: Conflict resolution — mediation basics and the conversations that don't happen often enough

**Week 3 — Scale and Complexity**

- Day 11: Organizational design — how structure shapes behavior, and what to change when you scale
- Day 12: Change management — how to move an organization without breaking it
- Day 13: Cross-functional collaboration — working across teams with different incentives
- Day 14: Role-play 2 — a multi-party negotiation with competing interests
- Day 15: Leadership memo — your operating principles, documented and defended

## What You'll Make

- A team charter and role map for a real or hypothetical team, with decision rights made explicit
- A negotiation plan and post-mortem from a structured role-play
- A leadership memo articulating your operating principles with concrete examples

## Key Practices

- Write it down before the meeting, not after — clarity happens in advance, not retrospect
- Prepare every negotiation: know your interests, your BATNA, and your walk-away before you sit down
- Separate the person from the problem — principled disagreement is a feature, not a bug
