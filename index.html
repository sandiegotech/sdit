How it works

Open a lesson. Think. Write. Come back.

No account, no enrollment, no cost. The site remembers where you left off on this device, and every lesson is built to be returned to — this year, next year, in five years.

1 · One lesson a day

The curriculum runs in daily lessons — a focused hour of reading, watching, and thinking. Follow the schedule in order, or wander the library and follow your curiosity. Both are correct.

2 · Write on the page

Lessons ask you real questions and give you space to answer right there in the page. Your words save automatically in your browser — and if you fork the repo, they save to files you own forever.

3 · Your work becomes a portfolio

Nothing here can be answered by copying a machine — the questions are about your life, your projects, your judgment. What accumulates is proof of effort: a record worth revisiting to see how your own mind has changed.

The premise

The Post-Industrial Renaissance.

Industrial-era education was built to produce specialists for a world of stable professions. That world is ending. The institutions that matter next will not be "AI universities" teaching tools that depreciate in eighteen months — they will be the ones that produce people whose value compounds as machine intelligence advances.

Technology, art, craft, science, and philosophy are one discipline, artificially divided by the industrial university. SDIT reunites them.

The bottega

Learning happens inside real work, under masters, in a workshop that ships. Every semester, every student is on a project team that builds something real — assessed by portfolio review and public exhibition, not seat time.

Bell Labs rigor

Deep technical rigor in service of building, not credentialing. Calculus, mechanics, linear algebra, machine learning — the full core, taught physical-before-formal, with periodic unassisted examinations. The institution certifies the human.

The waterman tradition

Physical mastery, courage, and judgment as part of the formation of the whole person. San Diego is the laboratory: the ocean teaches physics, the culture teaches media, the terrain teaches engineering.

The dual register

Keep the names, transform the delivery. The catalog says Mechanics, Calculus I, Probability & Statistics — the classroom is Scripps Pier, the ramp, the recording studio. The credential is the interface; the portfolio is the truth.

What SDIT certifies

Five outcomes, assessed at annual gates.

Judgment — rigorous reasoning under uncertainty. Command of Intelligence — directing, building, and evaluating AI systems as a native medium. Craft — conceiving, designing, and physically realizing working artifacts. Articulation — writing and speaking with clarity and force. Breadth of Mind — history, philosophy, literature, science, and living culture as one continuous conversation.

Every year ends in a gate: portfolio review plus written and oral examination against all five. The portfolio is the primary artifact.

Open courseware

The open curriculum.

Free, self-paced lessons published as open courseware — 75 daily lessons across philosophy, writing, mathematics, science, and creative practice, plus a library of 40 courses.
